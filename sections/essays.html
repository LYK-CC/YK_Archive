Essays & Research Projects

I began coding in fourth grade, starting with Scratch and later moving on to C. I started on nothing more than a Windows XP computer. Competing among many talented students in Asia, I had barely advanced to USACO Platinum by my ninth grade. But I soon realized that code felt empty without real-world application, which led me to the world of Mathematical Modeling.

Below is a curated collection of my involvement in IMMC, MCM/ICM, and SJMMA competition papers, where I explored real-world challenges through simulations, modeling, and data-driven analysis.
